Shark Tank Rules

×

Description: Participants are invited to present their innovative ideas or projects, which may pertain to various fields. This event provides a platform for aspiring entrepreneurs to pitch their concepts, ongoing initiatives, or completed projects and potentially secure funding.

Registration Fees: Nil

Prize Pool: Rs 15,000

Presentation: Participants can present innovative ideas, ongoing projects, or completed initiatives within a specified time limit.

Field of Ideas/Projects: Ideas and projects from various domains are welcome, with no specific emphasis on any particular field.

Team: A team can contain upto 5 participants.

Time Limit: Each presentation should not exceed a specified time limit (e.g., 5 minutes) for pitching their idea or project.

Judging Criteria: Ideas and projects will be evaluated based on innovation, feasibility, impact, market potential, and presentation skills.

Prize Allocation: The prize pool of Rs 15,000 will be distributed among the top-performing ideas or projects as determined by the judges.

Ownership: Participants retain ownership of their ideas or projects. Investors, if interested, can negotiate terms separately.

Feedback: Constructive feedback will be provided to all participants to help them refine their concepts or projects.

Registration: Participants must register in advance to secure a slot for their presentation.

Decision: The decision of the judges and potential investors will be final.

Intellectual Property: Participants are responsible for protecting their intellectual property if necessary.

Confidentiality: The event organizers and judges will respect the confidentiality of the ideas or projects presented.

Code of Conduct: Participants are expected to adhere to a code of conduct that promotes respect and professionalism during the event.

● THERE WILL BE NO PARTICIPATION FEE.

Coding Challenge Rules

Date:12th October 2023
Registration Fees: Nil
Prize Pool: Rs. 20,000
Mode: Offline
Event Description: The BVICAM coding competition is structured into two rounds, designed to assess participants' technical proficiency. The initial round is the preliminary stage, featuring a series of multiple-choice questions (MCQs). Subsequently, the final round is centered on coding challenges. During both rounds, participants are equipped with computer systems equipped with internet connectivity, enabling them to demonstrate their technical prowess and problem-solving skills in a competitive environment.
Participants attending the event on the college premises will have access to systems with high-speed internet.

Round 1: Quiz (Prelims)

Time: Prelims will be held from 9:30 am to 12:30 pm.
Format: Quiz with 30 questions.
Time Limit: 20 minutes.
Eligibility: Open to all participants.
Instructions: Participants can choose any time between 9:30 am and 12:30 pm to attempt the quiz, but they have only one attempt.
Results: Results will be announced at 2 pm.

Round 2: Coding Round

Time: Round 2 will start at 3 pm.
Format: Coding round with 3 questions.
Time Limit: 2 hours.
Programming Language: Participants can use any programming language.
Judging Criteria: Participants will be evaluated based on submission time and the complexities of their solutions.

UI/UX Rules

Design Heist: Stages and Timeline

Round 1: Logo making round
The registered individuals/teams will get the theme details and they would be required to make the logo in the given time. Their logo should be in accordance with the theme strictly and should not be copied from online sources. The theme will be revealed soon.

Round 2: UI/UX round
The shortlisted individuals/teams will have to make a design on the given theme. Their design should be in accordance with the theme strictly and should not be copied from online sources.

About Design Heist
IEEE BVCOE, the student branch of Bharati Vidyapeeth's college of engineering, New Delhi, in collaboration with BVICAM, is organizing its flagship event, Design Heist, an offline logo making and UI/UX competition, on October 12, 2023, as part of IEEE Day celebrations. The competition is divided into two rounds: The logo making round and then the UI/UX round. Each round would be of 2 hours, and only the shortlisted individuals/teams would be entering the final round, which is the UI/UX round. The themes would be announced soon.

General Guidelines:
- Any individual/team found to be cheating, interfering with another individual/team, or the competition in any form will be punished with severe point deductions and/or can be immediately disqualified from the competition.
- Plagiarism of any form will not be tolerated.
- The size of the team can be 1 to 2 members.
- There is no participation fee for the event.
- To participate in the event, all individuals/team members must be enrolled in an undergraduate program.
- Any submission with irregularities in commit history or utilization of premade content will lead to disqualification.
